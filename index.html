Welcome to 300eloChess

Chess is a classic strategy board game played between two opponents. The game requires skill, tactical thinking, and strategic planning. Each piece has unique movements and abilities, making chess a game of infinite possibilities.

The objective is simple: checkmate your opponent's king. But getting there requires careful planning, foresight, and understanding of piece movements and tactics.

Chess Pieces & Movements

Pawn

Movement: Moves forward one square (or two on its first move). Captures diagonally forward.

Special: Promotes to any other piece when reaching the opposite end.
‚ôû

Knight

Movement: Moves in an L-shape: 2 squares in one direction and 1 square perpendicular. Can jump over other pieces.

Special: The only piece that can jump over others.

‚ôù

Bishop

Movement: Moves diagonally any number of squares. Each player starts with one light-squared and one dark-squared bishop.

Special: Bishops always stay on the same color square.

‚ôú

Rook

Movement: Moves horizontally or vertically any number of squares.

Special: Important for endgame play and castling move.

‚ôõ

Queen

Movement: Moves any number of squares horizontally, vertically, or diagonally. The most powerful piece.

Special: Can move in any direction.

‚ôö

King

Movement: Moves one square in any direction. The most important piece.

Special: When the king is in checkmate, the game ends.
